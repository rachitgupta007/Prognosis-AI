Rigorous forecasting for the most critical transition in human history.

Prognosis AI was founded on the belief that the arrival of Transformative Artificial Intelligence (TAI) is an empirical question, not a philosophical one.

Our Mandate

To provide policymakers and the public with falsifiable, probability-weighted timelines for AI capability milestones.

The Hypothesis

We operate under the "Biological Anchor" hypothesis, which suggests that the computational complexity of the human brain provides a soft upper bound for the compute required to train a transformative AI model. By combining this with empirical trends in hardware efficiency (Moore's Law, interconnect scaling) and algorithmic efficiency, we can construct probability distributions for TAI arrival.

Methodology

Our approach differs from standard economic forecasting. We do not simply extrapolate GDP. Instead, we model the inputs of intelligence production:

  • → Compute Availability (FLOPs)
  • → Energy Constraints (Gigawatts)
  • → Algorithmic Efficiency (Halving time)
  • → Data Scarcity (Token exhaustion)

We use Bayesian updates to refine these models as new large-scale training runs are released. Every major model release (e.g., GPT-4, Gemini Ultra, Claude 3) provides a data point that collapses our uncertainty range.

Why It Matters

If TAI arrives within the next decade, current governance frameworks are woefully inadequate. Our goal is not to accelerate or decelerate progress, but to illuminate the trajectory so that society can navigate the transition with eyes wide open.
