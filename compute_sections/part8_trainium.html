The Utilitarian: Amazon Trainium & The Cost Curve

PART 8: FERRARI VS CAMRY

If you stand next to an NVIDIA Blackwell rack, you feel the arrogance of the silicon. It is the Ferrari SF90 of compute. It screams "Oomph."

But walk into Project Rainier—AWS’s cluster in Oregon—and the vibe is different. It smells like logistics. Here, 500,000 Trainium 2 chips are humming. They are not Ferraris. They are Toyota Camrys. They are boring, beige, and possessed of a singular competence.

The Physics: The Systolic Heartbeat

Trainium 2 is a scalpel. It strips away the "General Purpose" baggage of a GPU. It has 8 NeuronCores (Systolic Arrays) and 96GB of HBM3e. It isn't faster than an H100. But it is sufficient.

The Economics: The Equity Wash

Why did Anthropic bet on Amazon? Because of the "Equity Wash." Amazon invested $8 billion in Anthropic, but they didn't hand over cash. They handed over compute credits. Anthropic spends those credits renting Trainium instances. Amazon's marginal cost to run those instances is low. Effectively, Amazon bought equity for the cost of electricity.

The Software: The "Jank" Moat

Why hasn't everyone switched? Because the software (Neuron SDK) is hard. It hates dynamic shapes. It hates chaos. To use it, you need a team of engineers to write kernel-level code. For a startup, this is a nightmare. For Anthropic, it is a moat. They pay the "Jank Tax" upfront to unlock massive long-term savings.
