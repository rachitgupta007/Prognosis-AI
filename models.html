Our Predictive Frameworks.

We maintain three primary models that feed into our aggregate forecast: Compute Scaling, Algorithmic Efficiency, and Energy Constraints.

Model A

Compute-Centric Scaling

This model assumes that intelligence is a function of compute (FLOPs) and data. It projects future hardware capabilities based on TSMC roadmaps and GPU architecture improvements.

Key Insight: We are currently seeing a 10x increase in training compute every 18 months, far outpacing Moore's Law due to massive capital investment.

Model B

Algorithmic Efficiency

Hardware is only half the story. Our research indicates that algorithmic improvements are effectively halving the compute required to reach a specific performance benchmark every 8-12 months.

Key Insight: The "effective compute" available to researchers is growing much faster than the raw hardware specs suggest.

Model C

Energy Constraints

The limiting factor for the next decade will likely be power availability. This model projects the maximum possible cluster size based on grid capacity, nuclear build-out timelines, and cooling requirements.

Key Insight: We project a "Power Wall" in 2028 where local grid capacity becomes the primary bottleneck for training runs > 1e29 FLOPs.
